One of the perks of loving an American girl is that you get to celebrate Thanksgiving twice each year. Such was the case this year as I took a couple of days off last week to wander across the border to Pennsylvania to spend the American Thanksgiving with Alicia and her family.

Thanksgiving is a really big holiday in the US, much more so than in Canada. Generally, the family get-togethers seem to be larger. Cousings, aunts, uncles and what not get in the mix. At the Heinle household, some gifts are even exchanged and a family football game is planned.

This year, not everyone wanted to get involved because it rained the entire day and the field was a muddy mess. As such, we ended up with only five willing participants: Alicia, Cousins Tim and Mike, myself and Alicia's father. We ended up playing some two on two while Alicia's father just roamed and played offense. We had fun and got rather... dirty.
